# Retail_Sales_Analytics_SQL
Performed end-to-end retail sales analysis using MySQL to evaluate product performance, customer purchase behavior, and monthly revenue trends. Applied data cleaning, customer segmentation, repeat purchase analysis, and MoM growth calculations to generate actionable business insights for marketing, inventory planning, and customer retention.

🧠 Project Title

Retail Sales Analytics Using SQL

📌 Project Overview

This project uses SQL to analyze retail sales data to improve sales performance, customer engagement, and inventory management. The analysis focuses on identifying high-performing products, customer behavior patterns, and sales trends.

🎯 Business Objectives

1) Identify top and low performing products
2) Understand customer purchase behavior
3) Measure customer loyalty
4) Track monthly sales trends
5) Enable targeted marketing through segmentation

🗂️ Datasets Used

1) sales_transaction
2) product_inventory
3) customer_profiles

🔧 Tools & Technologies

1) MySQL 8+
2) SQL (Window Functions, CTEs, Aggregations)

📊 Key Analysis Performed

1) Data Cleaning (duplicates, null handling, date cleaning)
2) Product Performance Analysis
3) Customer Segmentation
4) Repeat Purchase Analysis
5) Month-on-Month Growth
6) Customer Loyalty Measurement

🚀 Business Impact

This analysis helps the company:
1) Focus marketing on high-revenue products
2) Identify slow-moving inventory
3) Target high-value customers
4) Improve customer retention
5) Monitor business growth trends

📈 Key Findings
🔥 Product Insights

1) Top products contribute majority of revenue
2) Few products show very low movement
3) Certain categories dominate total sales

👥 Customer Insights

1) High-frequency customers generate most revenue
2) Some customers show repeat purchase behavior
3) Many customers fall into low purchase segment

📅 Sales Trend Insights

1) Sales show monthly fluctuation
2) Certain months have peak revenue
3) MoM growth helps track business momentum

🎯 Recommendations

1) Promote low-performing products
2) Reward high-value customers
3) Improve retention for occasional buyers
4) Maintain inventory for top-selling products
